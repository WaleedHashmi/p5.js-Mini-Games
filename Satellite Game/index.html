WHY IS SPACE EXPLORATION IMPORTANT?

Technological Advancements

Space Exploration has enabled humans to excel in fields of medicine and science. Space exploration has contributed to many diverse aspects of everyday life, from solar panels to implantable heart monitors, from cancer therapy to light‐weight materials, and from water‐purification systems to improved computing systems and to a global search‐and‐rescue system.

Protection from Asteroids

About one time every 10,000 years, an asteroid the size of a football field smashes into Earth. Our solar system has calmed down a lot since the early eons, but there are still uncountable big asteroids and comets out there that could potentially cause mass extinction. A robust space program can help detect and deflect asteroids before any catastrophic damage.

Colonization in Space

Even though Earth is our home and will be for the next few centuries at least, it has been heavily impacted by climate change. While we must take good care of our planet, we cannot ignore the possibility that it might not be habitable one day. Space exploration makes sure that we get one step closer to space colonization every day. Life on Mars is slowly becoming a reality

Mining in Outer Space

Excavation of metals, minerals and other valuable raw materials has put a strain of natural resources which are not renewable. Mining on asteroids and on the moon for materials like Gold, Silver, Platinum can alleviate the burden on Earth's natural resources and would guarantee a much longer and stable supply of precious minerals.

Monitoring the Climate on Earth

Climate monitoring satellites have enabled humans to track hurricanes, sandstorms and tsunamis before they cause any damage allowing settlements to be relocated. The effects of global warming including rising sea levels and temperatures can also be monitored using these satellites.

CONTRIBUTE, DON'T LET GRAVITY HOLD YOU BACK!

Zooniverse

A citizen science project hosting a league of science projects which can benefit with your active participation. Explore the moon and the sun or study the wind patterns on the Martian surface.

SETI@Home

The Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence project allows you to donate your idle computer time to performa a small part of a larger more complicated calculation.

Einstein@Home

Makes use of the same idle computer time and distributed computing principles to search for pulsars from spinning neutron stars.

INSPIRE Project

Allows you to measure very low frequency emissions with a radio kit and help scientists resolve scientific mysteries.

Flight Analogs

This program seeks volunteers from the general population who can help the space scientists study the effects of space on the human body.

Citizen Sky

Observe epsilon Aurigae, send in your observations, see your results, analyze them and publish them in a scientific journal.
